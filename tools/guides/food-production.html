🍖 Increasing Food Production

Guide Economy Food ~4 min read

Food is the number that quietly caps your army. Every troop and tool sitting in a castle eats food each hour, and your net food (production − consumption) decides whether you can keep recruiting or watch your soldiers starve. This is how to push the production side up — and, just as importantly, how to stop bleeding it on the consumption side.

How food actually works

  • It's per-castle, not global. Each Main Castle, Outpost, Kingdom castle and metropolis has its own farms, its own production and its own consumption. A starving outpost won't be saved by a surplus back home.
  • Production is a base × (1 + bonuses). Your farmhouses set the base food/hour; decorations, buildings, build items and a few commanders multiply it.
  • Consumption scales with what's parked there. Troops and tools each have a food upkeep. A full courtyard of high-tier units can eat more than a maxed farm row produces — so the fastest "food fix" is often moving or merging armies, not building more farms.
  • Negative net food isn't instant death. You burn stored food first; only when the granary hits zero do units start dying. Use that buffer deliberately during events.

The quick version — push production up

  • Farmhouses: your base. Build the full row and keep them at max level for the kingdom — Green (Great Empire) farms out-produce the special-kingdom equivalents, so prioritise food there and import.
  • Decoration cap: food-production decos stack up to the +1500% food production cap. Big event/gacha decos give hundreds of % each; fill toward the cap before chasing anything else.
  • Buildings: the Storehouse raises your food cap (lets you bank a bigger buffer), and the Tower / Granary upgrades add storage. Storage doesn't make food, but a bigger buffer is what lets you run net-negative through an event without losses.
  • Build items: permanent food decorations from offers/events, plus temporary gatsha "harvest"-type items (big % food for a few days). Slot the temporary ones right before you expect heavy consumption.
  • Prosperity & taxes feed coin, not food — don't confuse the two. Food only comes from farms × food bonuses.

The other half — cut consumption

  • Park armies smart. Keep your big defensive stack in one castle rather than smeared across outposts, and put it where your food bonuses are highest.
  • Tools eat too. Mountains of unused siege tools sitting in a castle still draw upkeep — store or use them.
  • Use the granary buffer for events. Before a big recruit dump or a defence wave, let food go net-negative on purpose and ride the stored buffer down, then top farms / slot a temp food item to recover.

Rule of thumb: hit the +1500% deco cap, keep farmhouses maxed in your best food kingdom, and treat the storehouse buffer as a resource you spend during events — not a line you must never cross.
